What is Padron?
Padron in English is Census. Empadronarse is the term for registering for the Padron, much like registering in the UK at the local borough where you live. Empadronarse is a crucial step before you can even apply for Residency in Fuerteventura. This is to let the municipality know that you (and your household) live within that municipality. This is also to serve as proof when applying for residency that you have a registered address in Fuerteventura.
There are other reasons why it's important to register for Padron, like getting discounts on travel between Cananry Islands and mainland Spain and be able to vote in local elections. You can find out more about it on Fuerteventura Now.

The Process

For us, the process was very easy and straightforward. Of course, every municipality have their own rules like booking an appointment first, but our local town hall has a walk-in service. We just had to turn up early enough to avoid the queues. Once we got to the counter for the registration service, we struggled a little bit with the communication as the clerk did not speak much English (although, she did try her best!) and we don't know a lot of Spanish either 😂!
Luckily, we brought our neighbour along with us who speaks a bit of Spanish and was able to help us out.

The Required Documents

There are only 3 (main) documents required for the registration.

  • No. 1 - Proof of address

    As we own a property, we only needed to show the Deed Title, so this was very straighforward for us. Although, when we were doing our research, we did come across some information about being able to register even if you're only renting a property. Of course, this involves rental contract and in some cases, a letter from the landlord or house owner. Check out this blog from a Spanish law firm to find out more about empadronarse if you are renting a property.
    If you want to know more about buying a property, please check out my Buying a property in Fuerteventura from the UK blog.

  • No. 2 - Passports

    Again, this was straightforward. As it is just myself and my husband who will be permanently living there, we just brought our original passports with us. Some people have advised us to bring photocopies of the data page as well, but our local town hall did not require these and the clerk took photocopies right then and there.

  • No. 3 - NIE

    What is NIE? It is a unique identification number for foreign citizens and is a requirement to be able to conduct any kind of legal procedures in Spain, such as buying a property and/or opening a bank account. For us, we got our NIE through our lawyer when we bought our property. They applied for our NIE on our behalf to be able to open a bank account for us as part of the process of buying a property. We did find some information on this Spanish law firm website on how to get one. In my opinion, if we didn't already have the NIE to start with, this would have been the very first thing that we would have had to sort out before anything else because without it, we would not be able to open a bank account, we would not be able to buy a property (or rent one) which means we couldn't register at the town hall.

  • No. 4 - Registration Form

    The form was only available at the town hall and was given to us to be filled out when we got there.

Conclusion

The process was very easy and straightforward for us. As usual, we first did our research and found out more about our local town hall as all municipalities have their differences. We also came prepared with all the documents we needed to bring and even brought someone with us who would help with the language barrier. 😊
